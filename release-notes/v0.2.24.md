# Meetflow v0.2.24

**Released:** 2026-04-27

## What's new

- **Dictation mode.** Press a global hotkey (default ⌃⌥Space) and speak into any focused text field — Mail, Slack, your editor, a chat window. By default, one press starts dictation and a second press stops it; if you'd rather, switch to hold-to-talk in Settings. You can cap individual dictations between 1 and 10 minutes (3 minutes by default), and a small overlay shows your live audio level while you speak.
- **Speaker labels in transcripts.** Meetings now automatically separate "Speaker 1", "Speaker 2", and so on, so the transcript reads like a script rather than a wall of text.
- **Batch transcribe.** A new Batch tab lets you drag in a folder or a stack of audio files and transcribe them all at once. You can also point it at a watched folder so anything you drop in transcribes automatically. The queue picks up where it left off if you quit and relaunch.
- **Transcribe from a URL.** Paste a YouTube or podcast link and Meetflow will download and transcribe it locally — same privacy guarantees as live recording, nothing leaves your Mac.
- **Settings, regrouped.** What used to be three crowded tabs is now seven (General, Audio, Transcription, Translation, Dictation, Integrations, About) so each control is easier to find.
- **Reset to defaults.** A single button in Settings → Transcription undoes every tweak in one click if you want to start over.
- **Pause on silence.** Optional setting that pauses live recording during long quiet stretches and resumes when you start talking again.
- **Updated onboarding.** First-run tour now walks new users through dictation, batch and URL transcription, and speaker labels.

## Fixes

- Fixed a build-pipeline issue where the anti-hallucination filter was missing from installed copies of v0.2.23. With it back in place, transcripts will once again suppress the recurring whisper artifacts ("Thank you", "Thanks for watching") that sometimes appeared during silent stretches.
- Pressing Escape now reliably cancels a dictation, even when Meetflow itself is the frontmost app.
- The Back button in onboarding no longer leaves you stranded on a step you can't advance past.

## Under the hood

- Hardened the MCP server's path validation against symlinked-folder bypasses (defense in depth — no exploit was known in the wild).
- Tightened privacy markers on log statements that touch transcribed audio, so transcript snippets no longer surface in `Console.app`.
- Fixed a small race in dictation finalization where pressing Escape during the transcription handoff could leave internal state inconsistent.
